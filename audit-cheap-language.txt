======================================================
CRITICAL: legora/index.html
======================================================

These are the worst offenders. The analysis is sophisticated but the
sourcing language actively undermines it.

  "I wrote a 23-page regulatory intelligence report using public data,
   AI tools I developed, and a mapping of their leadership."
   → "using public data" reads as "I googled this"

  "What the Public Data Shows (as of April 30, 2026)"
   → Section header. Screams database scraping.

  "Searched 57 DOL LCA/PERM disclosure files (FY2008–FY2026 Q1)
   and USCIS H-1B Employer Data Hub."
   → "Searched...disclosure files" is the exact problem.

  "DOL LCA + PERM Disclosure Data · All available years FY2008–FY2026 Q1
   · Direct database search"
   → "Direct database search" should never appear anywhere.

  "The Department of Labor's disclosure datasets -- covering every
   H-1B, E-3, H-1B1, and PERM filed in the US..."
   → Naming DOL as source in body text.

  "Not in DOL data." / "No LCA. No federal filing trail."
   → Fine as analytical insight but framed as database output.

  "What doesn't show up in DOL data: O-1A petitions carry no LCA
   requirement..."
   → Insight is strong, framing is weak.

  SOURCES SECTION (lines ~2153–2182):
   Lists "U.S. DOL LCA Disclosure Data," "U.S. DOL PERM Disclosure Data,"
   "USCIS H-1B Employer Data Hub," "USCIS Data Hub" repeatedly.
   → Bibliography looks like scraped government datasets.

  DISCLAIMER (line ~2188):
   "This brief is based exclusively on publicly disclosed DOL LCA and
   PERM data, USCIS public records, and publicly available company
   information."
   → The word "exclusively" makes it worse. Entire brief is undercut.

======================================================
THE CORE PROBLEM (ONE SENTENCE)
======================================================

The analysis is sophisticated. The sourcing language makes it look
like the sophistication came from searching government websites.

A strategic advisor does not explain that data came from
"DOL public disclosure files." That is assumed and unstated.
The emphasis on "public data" and "direct database search" signals
the method, not the insight — and the method is not impressive
on its own.


======================================================
RECOMMENDED REPLACEMENTS
======================================================

  "public data"
   → "regulatory intelligence" / "empirical labor market analysis"

  "DOL LCA public disclosure files"
   → "government authorization records" / remove entirely

  "Direct database search"
   → delete

  "Based on public filings"
   → "Empirical cost modeling" / "Conservative estimate"

  "Searched 57 DOL LCA/PERM disclosure files"
   → delete or move to methodology appendix, never in body text

  "based exclusively on publicly disclosed DOL LCA and PERM data"
   → "informed by regulatory program data, industry benchmarking,
     and peer program evaluation"

  "DOL LCA Disclosure Data, FY2015 to Q1 FY2026" (chart labels)
   → "Labor market analysis, FY2015–Q1 FY2026" / omit source

  "LCA compliance" (resume)
   → "immigration program governance"

  "Form I-9 completion, audit procedures, E-Verify administration"
   → "Established governance frameworks for workforce authorization
     compliance and audit readiness"
